Introduction

Hi. I'm an experienced SDET with knowledge about frontend and backend development. I started my testing journey in the hardware space, building and maintaining laboratories of routers, switches, POS systems and access points for AT&T's Network Operations Center. Once those systems were built, I spent most of my day troubleshooting with field technicians for large companies like McDonald's and Starbucks. I became a NOC manager and soon found out that I like creating and automating processes between my team and engineering as well as managing incidents and problem solving. This caught the attention of the QA department and I was invited to join a team of database-focused testing engineers.

There was only one problem, I had trained myself for potential jobs like networking or systems administration and had no experience with programming. I re-enrolled myself college and started learning Python, Java and C++ as well as taking online tutorials, attending hackathons, and joining coding challenges. But I noticed there wasn't an SDET group in Austin, so I grabbed two of my friends and we started Austin Automation Professionals. We quickly assembled some of the best speakers and QA professionals in the area, worked with other groups like Cloud Austin and DevOps Austin, and began a following of around 50 members who regularly showed up to our monthly meetings and ended with a total list of over 2500 members.

I continue to serve the local tech community as a co-organizer for DevOpsDays Austin and a project manager for Open Austin. I am also re-enrolled in Austin Community College with a focus on an associates in UX/UI design. Combined with my MBA, this gives me a unique but well-rounded software engineering background.
